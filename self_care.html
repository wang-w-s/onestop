Self-Care Tips
College, as fun as it may be at times, can also be extremely stressful for students. One way to help manage life as a student is to practice self-care. Self-care describes the actions taken to promote both physical and mental health, as well one's emotional well-being. Self-care activities may enhance one's quality of life and overall health.
1. Sleep
Sleep is important for our physical, mental, and immune health. It is also correlated with academic success. Try to get approximately 7-9 hours of sleep each night. Try to avoid all-nighters, as this can negatively affect a student's performance and and long term academic success.
2. Exercise
Research shows that exercise can relieve stress, improve your mood, and help you sleep better. Try to set some time aside during the week to get some exercise.
3. Do Something You Love
Allow yourself to spend time on the things you love, whether it's hiking, painting, dancing, sewing, or cooking. These hobbies can provide an outlet for stress and can increase happiness.
4. Practice Self-Compassion
Treat yourself like you would treat your best friend or a younger sibling. Talk to yourself the way you would talk to someone you care about and love. Try to talk to yourself with compassion rather than criticism.
5. Practice Deep Breathing
